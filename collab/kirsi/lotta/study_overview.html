Study overview

1 Research aim

Child diet at 2 years and 5-6 years is evaluated against the child plasma metabolome measured at the same ages, with an additional prospective analysis from 2-year diet to 5-6-year metabolites.

Questions:

  1. concurrent associations at 2 years
  2. concurrent associations at 5-6 years
  3. prospective associations from 2-year diet to 5-6-year metabolites, conditioning on the same metabolite at 2 years
  4. repeated-measures associations across 2 years and 5-6 years

6 Multiple testing

False-discovery control is based on Benjamini-Hochberg adjustment across metabolites, but the adjustment scope differs by model family.

  • Concurrent outputs apply BH within each exposure-specific result set.
  • Prospective outputs apply BH within each exposure-specific result set.
  • Repeated-measures outputs apply BH separately within each exposure and coefficient family.

      ifelse(n_complete_min == n_complete_max, as.character(n_complete_min), paste0(n_complete_min, "-", n_complete_max))

  ) %>%
  select(question, analysis, exposure, status, reason, n_complete, n_features_tested, term_family)

9 Interpretation notes

  1. The concurrent and prospective models carry the clearest inferential weight.
  2. The prospective model is conditional on the earlier metabolite value, so it should be interpreted as a follow-up association rather than a pure baseline-only prediction.
  3. The repeated-measures component is included because it is part of the Study 1 question set, but it remains the most warning-prone analysis family and should be interpreted more cautiously than the linear components.
  4. Detailed coefficient tables, sensitivity comparisons, and supporting figures are reported elsewhere in the site; this page is the study-level map.
